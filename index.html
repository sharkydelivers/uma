0 Background

When you place a bet on Polymarket — say, "Will Israel and Hezbollah reach a ceasefire by April 18?" — someone has to decide whether it happened. For international users, that decision is made by UMA's Optimistic Oracle. UMA token holders vote on the outcome. Majority wins. Losers get slashed.

The system was designed to be decentralized: anyone can buy UMA tokens and vote. In theory, thousands of independent voters reach consensus. In practice, we traced exactly who votes and how much power they hold.

What we found: one company controls the majority.

Risk Labs — the company that built UMA — owns 86 proxy voting contracts through a single multisig wallet signed by their CEO. Combined with their Treasurer's personal stake and funded wallets, the Risk Labs-aligned bloc holds 11.37 million UMA = 64.4% of all staked tokens.

They earn $665,000 per year in staking rewards. They agree 99% of the time across thousands of votes. And the system punishes anyone who disagrees with them.

No one has published this before. Media has covered individual incidents. We traced the entire system.

How to read this document: Every claim includes an Etherscan link, subgraph query, or on-chain proof. Nothing here requires trust — only verification. If you see something wrong, you can check it yourself in 5 minutes.

1 86 Proxy Contracts, One Owner

UMA has a feature called DesignatedVotingV2 — a proxy contract that holds UMA and votes on behalf of its owner. There's a public factory that creates them. Between February 2023 and February 2026, this factory produced 89 contracts.

We didn't sample. We checked every single one.

How we checked

Each DV2 contract has a function holdsRole(uint256 roleId, address account). When roleId = 0 (Owner), it returns TRUE if that address owns the contract. We called this on all 89 contracts with the Safe address 0x8180d59b7175d4064bdfa8138a58e9babffda44a.

Results

ResultCountDetails
TRUE (Safe owns it)86All owned by the same multisig
FALSE3#1: owned by Risk Labs Deployer directly. #56, #78: independent users.

87 of 89 contracts (97.8%) are in the Risk Labs ecosystem. 86 owned by the Safe, plus 1 owned by their labeled deployer.

The deployment pattern

On February 26, 2023, 33 contracts were deployed in 27 minutes across 5 consecutive blocks. All via multicall(). Four days later, the Safe distributed 17,025,000 UMA to 29 contracts in a single transaction.

This isn't a gradual community adoption. It's an infrastructure deployment.

Verify yourself: Go to any DV2 contract (e.g. 0x0afc9a935960948ed35e50ac7639cbb38e840b38). Click "Read as Proxy." Call holdsRole(0, 0x8180d59b7175d4064bdfa8138a58e9babffda44a). Returns: true.

When two wallets vote identically on EVERY request in a round, they receive mathematically identical per-token reward rates. We found Kevin Chan and Wallet #3 have identical reward rates to 10 decimal places in 89.9% of rounds. Wallet #3 and #5 match in 85.5%. All 5 simultaneously match in 61.1%.

9 The Slashing Trap

UMA's slashing mechanism is designed to punish wrong voters. In practice, it creates an unbreakable feedback loop:

Vote with the majority → earn 23% APR

Vote with the minority → lose 0.1% of your stake per round

The majority IS Risk Labs (64.4% of stake)

The result:

  • Cost to challenge a resolution: $4.54M+ in UMA (enough to overcome the bloc) — plus risk losing it all if you fail
